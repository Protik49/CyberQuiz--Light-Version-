Major Cybersecurity Scandals in History

The Equifax Data Breach

September 2017

One of the largest data breaches in history affected 147 million people. Hackers exploited a vulnerability in Equifax's system to steal sensitive personal data including Social Security numbers, birth dates, addresses, and driver's license numbers.

SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack

December 2020

Attackers compromised SolarWinds' software development supply chain, inserting malware into legitimate software updates. This affected up to 18,000 organizations including multiple U.S. government agencies.

Colonial Pipeline Ransomware Attack

May 2021

The largest fuel pipeline in the U.S. was forced to shut down due to a ransomware attack, causing fuel shortages across the East Coast. The company paid a $4.4 million ransom to restore operations.

Yahoo Data Breaches

2013-2014 (disclosed in 2016)

Two massive data breaches affected all 3 billion Yahoo user accounts, making it the largest data breach in history. The stolen information included names, email addresses, phone numbers, and hashed passwords.
